About Corona Virus

COVID-19 is a highly contagious viral respiratory illness caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It was first identified in December 2019 in Wuhan, China, and has since become a global pandemic. Symptoms can range from mild to severe, including fever, cough, and difficulty breathing. Preventive measures such as wearing masks, social distancing, and vaccination have been crucial in managing its spread

Symptoms

The symptoms of COVID-19 can vary widely and may appear 2 to 14 days after exposure to the SARS-CoV-2 virus. Common symptoms include:

  • Fever or chills
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Fatigue
  • Muscle or body aches

Mild Case:

In a mild case of COVID-19, an individual may experience mild respiratory symptoms similar to the common cold or flu. Symptoms can include a mild cough, low-grade fever, fatigue, headache, and body aches. Some individuals may also experience a loss of taste or smell. Severe Case:

In a severe case of COVID-19, an individual experiences more intense symptoms and may develop pneumonia or acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS). Severe cases are more likely to occur in older adults and individuals with underlying health conditions, such as heart disease, diabetes, or compromised immune systems. Symptoms may include high fever, severe cough, shortness of breath, chest pain, confusion, and bluish lips or face. Asymptomatic Case:

In some cases of COVID-19, individuals may be asymptomatic, which means they show no symptoms despite being infected with the virus. Asymptomatic individuals can still spread the virus to others, making them potential carriers of COVID-19 without realizing it. Asymptomatic cases pose challenges in containing the spread of the virus, as infected individuals may unknowingly transmit it to vulnerable populations.
